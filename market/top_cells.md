## Cell 1 (Priority Rank: 1)
**Sector**: AI Infrastructure / MLOps
**Company Size**: 15–80 employees
**AI Readiness**: Score 2–3

**Cell Population**: 42 companies in Crunchbase ODM sample
**Average Funding (12 months)**: $14.2M
**Average Hiring Velocity**: +87% (engineering roles in 60 days)
**Bench Match Score**: 0.91

**Cell Profile**: This is the highest-priority outbound cell for Tenacious. These are post-Series A/B AI infrastructure companies that are actively hiring ML engineers and platform engineers — the exact skills on the Tenacious bench. They have confirmed budget (recent funding), confirmed need (high job velocity, AI-adjacent roles), and confirmed technical readiness to engage on AI infrastructure conversations (maturity score 2–3). The typical bottleneck: they're trying to hire 4–6 ML engineers simultaneously, in-house recruiting takes 3–4 months per hire, and post-funding runway pressure means they can't wait.

**Specific Outbound Recommendation**: 30 emails/week from this cell. Use Segment 1 + Segment 4 overlay messaging. Lead with job-post velocity signal (if ≥5 roles) and the ML platform gap comparison. Target CTO/VP Engineering directly.

**False Positive Risk**: Companies that raised AI infrastructure funding but are actually building tooling for other companies' AI (i.e., developer tools, not enterprise AI adoption). These companies need to hire AI engineers, not buy outsourced AI capacity. Screening filter: require B2B enterprise sales signal in Crunchbase description.

## Cell 2 (Priority Rank: 2)
**Sector**: Enterprise SaaS (B2B)
**Company Size**: 50–200 employees
**AI Readiness**: Score 2–3

**Cell Population**: 68 companies in Crunchbase ODM sample
**Average Funding (12 months)**: $22.4M
**Average Hiring Velocity**: +45%
**Bench Match Score**: 0.78

**Cell Profile**: Mid-market B2B SaaS companies that have decided to build AI features into their product (maturity score 2–3) and are now scaling their engineering team to deliver on the AI roadmap. The funding is larger (Series B/C) but hiring velocity is moderate — these companies are more disciplined about hiring than the AI-infra cell. The buying trigger is usually a combination of product demand for AI features (customers asking for AI capabilities) and board pressure to show AI differentiation. Tenacious bench match is strong on Python/data but slightly weaker on domain-specific ML.

**Specific Outbound Recommendation**: 20 emails/week. Use Segment 1 messaging with AI maturity overlay. Lead with competitor gap analysis — what are top-quartile SaaS companies in their sector doing with AI that they're not? Focus on data platform and ML engineering roles specifically.

**Caution**: Many companies in this cell are in the "loud but shallow" AI maturity trap — lots of press about AI features, but actual ML team is small. Validate job-post signals carefully before asserting AI maturity.

## Cell 3 (Priority Rank: 3)
**Sector**: Fintech / Insurtech
**Company Size**: 30–150 employees
**AI Readiness**: Score 1–2

**Cell Population**: 54 companies
**Average Funding (12 months)**: $18.7M
**Average Hiring Velocity**: +38%
**Bench Match Score**: 0.71

**Cell Profile**: Post-Series A/B fintech companies that are beginning to invest in AI/ML (maturity score 1–2) and have confirmed funding. The AI maturity is lower than the top two cells, but the funding signal is strong and the typical buying trigger is competitive pressure — fintech companies that see competitors launching AI-powered features feel urgency to catch up. Bench match is good for Python data engineers and data platform work; weaker for domain-specific fintech ML (fraud detection, credit scoring) which requires specialized skills.

**Specific Outbound Recommendation**: 10 emails/week. Use Segment 1 messaging, AI maturity score 1–2 language ("scale your first AI function"). Be cautious about asserting AI maturity when score is 1 — use question framing. Check for layoff signals before outreach (fintech has been volatile; Segment 2 might be better angle for some companies in this cell).

**False Positive Risk**: AI maturity score 1 in fintech is often driven by regulatory reporting requirements (model explainability for compliance) rather than genuine AI investment. These companies need compliance-focused data engineers, not ML platform builders. Different value proposition.

## Cell 4 (Priority Rank: 4)
**Sector**: HealthTech / BioTech
**Company Size**: 20–100 employees
**AI Readiness**: Score 1–2

**Cell Population**: 31 companies
**Average Funding (12 months)**: $28.9M (highest average funding of any cell)
**Average Hiring Velocity**: +52%
**Bench Match Score**: 0.65

**Cell Profile**: Well-funded healthtech companies with large funding rounds and meaningful hiring velocity. The bench match score is lower (0.65) because healthtech ML requires domain expertise (clinical NLP, medical imaging, EHR data formats) that is not well-represented on the current Tenacious bench. Recommend this cell only when a specific health-domain engineer is available on bench.

**Specific Outbound Recommendation**: 5 emails/week, targeted only when bench has relevant health-domain ML capacity. Gate this cell on bench availability before opening outreach.

## Cell 5 (Priority Rank: 6 — Developer Tools)
**Sector**: DevTools / Infrastructure
**Company Size**: 15–60 employees
**AI Readiness**: Score 3

**Cell Population**: 28 companies
**Average Funding (12 months)**: $9.8M (lower — early stage)
**Average Hiring Velocity**: +112% (highest of any cell)
**Bench Match Score**: 0.88

**Cell Profile**: Small but high-maturity AI developer tools companies. Score 3 AI maturity with very high hiring velocity. However: these companies are building AI tools, not buying outsourced AI capacity. They need to hire AI engineers (and fast), but they're likely better candidates for Segment 4 project consulting (time-boxed delivery) than Segment 1 talent outsourcing. Also: funding is lower than other cells — these are often early-stage companies where Tenacious's minimum engagement (3–12 engineers, 6–24 months) might be too heavy.

**Specific Outbound Recommendation**: Prioritize Segment 4 project consulting pitch (ML platform work, agentic systems, evaluation harness). 5 emails/week. Reference specific technical work (GitHub activity, blog posts) in outreach — these CTOs will immediately spot generic outreach and ignore it.
